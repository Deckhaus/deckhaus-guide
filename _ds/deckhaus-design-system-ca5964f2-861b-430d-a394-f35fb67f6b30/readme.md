# Deckhaus — Design System

> Crafted presentations & visuals. Enhanced by AI.

Deckhaus is a boutique visual communication studio creating strategic presentations, brand experiences and content systems for brands that need to **present, launch and grow**. This repository is the studio's design system: brand foundations (color, type, spacing), reusable UI components, a marketing-site UI kit, and a deck/slide template set — everything an agent or designer needs to produce on-brand work.

The voice is **Swiss, premium, precise, editorial, contemporary and human**. AI is positioned as an accelerant for exploration and production — never as magic. Human judgement stays central: *"AI inspires, but the designer defines."*

## The studio in one minute

**Positioning.** The next evolution of the presentation-design market — boutique, strategic, system-based and AI-assisted. Not a generic "presentation design agency", not a SaaS startup, not outsourced PowerPoint production.

**Three systems. One visual language.** Deckhaus is organised around **three core systems**, not a catalogue of separate services. (The eight detailed offerings still exist internally, mapped underneath the three.)
1. **Strategic Presentations** *(core)* — high-stakes business communication: sales decks, pitch decks, investor materials, corporate reports, PowerPoint template systems, AI-assisted presentation workflows. The value is a **clearer sales narrative, consistent structure and faster adaptation** across teams and regions — not simply “better slides”.
2. **Content Systems** — reusable visual systems for consistent communication across channels: social storytelling, campaign frameworks, modular toolkits, monthly production. The value is a **scalable visual language a client team can use with confidence** — not “we make social posts”.
3. **Brand Experiences** — the visual layer behind launches, events and branded moments: event identity, keynote & screen content, motion direction, launch systems. Deckhaus owns the **creative and visual layer** (narrative, screens, decks, motion, brand presence), not technical AV/staging production.

**The strategic idea:** *Deckhaus doesn't simply design slides — it designs how a brand appears in front of an audience.*

**Core positioning.** *Deckhaus creates visual systems for brands that need to present, launch and grow.* Supporting line: *“We design the visual systems behind presentations, launches and brand experiences.”* Presentation design stays the commercial core; Content Systems and Brand Experiences are natural extensions of the same visual-communication philosophy.

**Visual Sprint™** — the studio's **operating philosophy**, not just a method. Its value isn't speed for its own sake: it's *making better visual decisions before production* — deciding how communication should look and feel before everything else is built, so what follows is more coherent, faster and easier to scale. AI accelerates exploration; human judgement defines the result. Five phases (Idea & Briefing → Prompt Design → AI Moodboard → Refine & Craft → Final Delivery), with the **Direction Room** as the validation moment — *alignment before execution*. Headline proof point: **70% less time on visual exploration**.

## POSITIONING & BRAND IDEA

The studio's point of view — what Deckhaus stands for, who it stands against, and the one idea the whole brand defends.

**The thesis (the studio's tesis propia).** *Most companies don't have a design problem. They have a communication-system problem.* They build a deck from scratch, then an event presentation from scratch, then a campaign from scratch, then a sales document from scratch. Each piece can be “fine”, yet nothing looks like part of the same visual thinking. Deckhaus is the answer to that fragmentation — not a studio that “does everything”, but one that **builds visual systems for the moments a brand needs to be especially clear, present and prepared.**

**Brand idea.** *Deckhaus doesn't just design pieces — it designs how a brand appears in front of an audience.* Whether it happens in a boardroom, a pitch, a giant screen, a launch or a LinkedIn feed, the underlying need is the same: a brand has to show up, explain something, hold attention, convince, align a team or mark a moment.

**Three scales of one idea.** Strategic Presentations, Content Systems and Brand Experiences are not three markets — they are three scales of the same problem:
- **Strategic Presentations — how a brand explains itself when it really matters.** The pitch, the sale, the executive committee, the investment conversation. Not pretty slides: clarity, narrative, structure, consistency and confidence in rooms where a weak presentation makes a strong idea look mediocre.
- **Content Systems — how a brand keeps that clarity when it isn't in an important meeting.** The everyday layer that stops communication becoming a string of disconnected pieces. Not “making posts”: a language that can repeat, adapt and grow without dropping a level.
- **Brand Experiences — how a brand becomes memorable when it appears before an audience.** Not technical event production — the **visual layer** of the moment: narrative, identity, screens, keynote content, motion language. How a brand stays coherent when it leaves a document and enters the real world.

**Positioning statement.** *Deckhaus builds visual systems for brands that need to present, launch and grow.* Supporting line: *“We design the visual systems behind presentations, launches and brand experiences.”* Not a deck shop, not outsourced PowerPoint, not a SaaS template tool, not a full technical event-production company. A studio with a thesis and the craft to back it.

**The declared enemy.** **Fragmentation** — communication built piece-by-piece from scratch, each one “fine” but never part of one thought. Its symptoms: the forgettable deck, template slop, decoration mistaken for design, raw AI output shipped as if novelty were quality, and brands that look like a different company on every surface.

**The dual-readability principle** *(the bar for every Deckhaus surface).* Each touchpoint must work on two levels at once:
- **Legible in 20 seconds** to a non-designer — a marketing director or founder grasps the value at a glance, in plain editorial language.
- **Instantly respectable** to a designer — typographic structure, Swiss grid, restraint and detailing earn craft credibility on sight.

**The deliberate ambiguity (the punch).** A viewer should not fully classify Deckhaus in ten seconds, but should feel it is clearly distinct in five. *“Is it a presentation studio? A brand agency? A visual consultancy? An experiences firm? Why does it seem to understand all of this as one thing?”* That small ambiguity is the point. **Deckhaus shouldn't look bigger than it is — it should look deeper than people expected.**

**What gives us authority.** A signature method (**Visual Sprint™**), a real system (this repository), senior-only hands, and proof (Lacoste, Café Vision, Northbank). Authority through evidence, never hype.

**The one-line POV.** *Most companies don't have a design problem. They have a communication-system problem — and Deckhaus builds the system.*

## CONTENT FUNDAMENTALS

How Deckhaus writes.

- **Voice:** editorial and confident, never salesy. Short declarative sentences. States capability plainly and lets the craft carry the persuasion.
- **Person:** "We" for the studio; "you / your team" for the client. Warm but professional — a senior collaborator, not a vendor.
- **Casing:** **sentence case** for headlines and UI by default. Reserve UPPERCASE for short mono eyebrows/labels (`OUR SERVICES`, `VISUAL SPRINT™`) and the occasional editorial statement pulled from source decks. The wordmark is **always lowercase**: `deckhaus`.
- **Punctuation & rhythm:** triads are a signature — *"Present. Launch. Grow."* · *"clear, coherent and visually striking"* · *"structure, speed and consistency."* Em-dashes for editorial asides. Periods used decisively, even on fragments.
- **Numbers & specifics:** concrete and quiet — `70%`, `5 phases`, `€3,000`, `CHF`, `01–08`. European/Swiss conventions (€, CHF, VAT-exclusive pricing). Tabular, mono-set figures.
- **AI language:** measured. AI "accelerates exploration", "reduces repetitive work", "supports better structure". Never "magic", "revolutionary", "10x", "level up", "supercharge". The principle line: *"AI should not replace judgement. It should reduce repetitive work and support better structure, speed and consistency."*
- **Emoji:** none. Ever.
- **Avoid:** "level up your presentations", generic agency catalogues, SaaS hype, futuristic AI clichés, social-media-agency speak, "stunning/amazing".

**On-brand snippets**
- "Where precision design meets artificial intelligence."
- "We transform ideas into intelligent, coherent and visually striking presentations."
- "Functional, elegant, and fully editable slide systems."
- "Not about replacing creativity — accelerating it."
- "Enhanced by AI — powered by human design."

## VISUAL FOUNDATIONS

The look: **warm-paper editorial surface with dark premium moments, Swiss grid, strong typography, generous spacing, one controlled accent.** Boutique design studio, not a dark SaaS product. The system is **paper-dominant** — roughly 55–60% warm off-white, 30–35% dark sections reserved for impact, 5–10% accent. Minimal but distinctive. No unnecessary decoration.

### Dark vs light — the rule
The marketing surface defaults to **warm paper**. Dark is an *emphasis tool*, not the baseline.
- **Dark sections** (`data-theme="dark"`, `background: var(--bg-base)`): hero moments, the Visual Sprint statement/process, strong CTAs, and any "system" showcase. Use sparingly for rhythm and impact.
- **Light sections** (default `data-theme="light"`, warm paper): case studies, services, work previews, editorial explanations, about/studio positioning, contact details.
- The nav and footer adapt: the **footer is always dark** (anchors the page); the **nav tone follows the first section** of each view (dark on Home/Visual Sprint, paper elsewhere). The mark switches white/black to suit.

### Color
- **Base is warm paper** `--paper #F4F4EF` on the marketing surface; the near-black warm ink ramp (`--ink-900 #0A0A0B` …) powers the dark impact sections and the app/deck UI. Within a dark section, surfaces step up through the ink ramp and elevation reads through **hairline borders**, not heavy shadow. Two themes share every token: default `data-theme="light"` (paper) and `data-theme="dark"` — either can be scoped to any section.
- **One accent: Deckhaus Chartreuse `--lime-500 #B7C94A`.** A refined, mature acid-olive — intelligent, precise and contemporary; fresh but never neon, premium but not boring. Dialled back from electric so it reads editorial, not technological. Used **once or twice per view**: an eyebrow tick, a single CTA, a stat unit, a selected emphasis word, a small detail. Never a large decorative block, never a gradient wash. On paper, accent *text* uses the darker `--lime-700` (via `--text-accent`) for legibility; the swatch/tick/CTA fill stays `--lime-500`. *(Token names remain `--lime-*` for stability.)*
- **Foreground is warm off-white** `--paper #F4F4EF`, not pure white — softer, more editorial on dark.
- **Functional signals** (`positive/negative/warning/info`) exist but are restrained — status only, never decoration.
- A **light editorial theme** (`[data-theme="light"]`) flips surfaces to paper/white for slides and print; the lime and ink ramp carry across.

### Typography

**System rules (non-negotiable):**
- **Tracking is `0` across the entire system.** Never negative, never compressed. Impact comes from scale, composition, spacing and contrast — not tightened letters. (Only small mono uppercase eyebrows carry positive tracking, `0.18em`, for legibility.)
- **Hector is Regular (400) only.** Never bold, never black, never faux-bold (no extra weight, shadow, stroke or compression to fake emphasis). Hector should read refined, editorial and premium. The display-weight token `--weight-display` is locked to 400.

- **Hector** — geometric display, Regular only. Hero statements, slide titles, big numbers, the wordmark. Tracking `0`; line-height `1.08–1.14` for large stacked headlines (token `--leading-tight: 1.1`). Calm, elegant, never crushed.
- **Geometria** — the workhorse sans (400/700). Body, UI, and bold section headings (bold weight is *Geometria's*, never Hector's). Clean and neutral with warmth.
- **IBM Plex Mono** — labels, eyebrows, indices (`01`), data, captions, metadata. Uppercase with wide tracking (`0.18em`) for eyebrows, line-height `1.4–1.5`. This mono-label layer is a core part of the editorial signal.
- **H1 rule:** Hector · weight 400 · tracking `0` · line-height `1.08–1.14` · controlled max-width · generous space between stacked lines. *"Present. Launch. Grow."* stacks one word per line with real breathing room — boutique editorial, not a compressed tech poster. "Grow." takes the acid-olive accent but stays fully readable.
- **Body** line-height `1.55–1.65` (token `--leading-normal: 1.6`); small copy `1.45–1.6`. Generous space between eyebrow → headline → body. Short, calm, readable blocks — no dense walls of mono.
- Scale is generous and editorial (16 → 108px). Headlines balance (`text-wrap: balance`); body wraps pretty.

### Layout & grid
- Precise Swiss grid; wide max-widths (`1280 / 1480`), generous page margins (`clamp(20px, 6vw, 96px)`) and section rhythm (`clamp(64px, 10vw, 160px)`).
- **Visible grid lines** as texture: faint `44–80px` grid overlays on hero/feature panels and slide visuals (`linear-gradient` hairlines at low opacity). This is the main "background graphic" — no photography stock, no blobs.
- Numbered structure everywhere (`01 / 05`, `01–08`) — lists, sections, process steps.
- 4px spacing grid.

### Surfaces, borders, radius, shadow
- **Cards:** `--surface-card` fill, `1px` hairline border, **modest radius** (`--radius-card 12px`; controls `8px`). Precise, not pillowy. Optional `2px` lime top-border to mark a featured card.
- **Radii are deliberately small** — Swiss precision. `sm 4 / md 8 / lg 12 / xl 18`. Pills only for tags/switches.
- **Shadows are subtle** (`shadow-md = 0 10px 28px rgba(0,0,0,.45)`); on dark, elevation leans on hairline borders rather than heavy shadow, and on paper a `1px` border + minimal shadow. **No accent glow** — the accent is a flat signal (tick, label, emphasis word, one CTA), never a bloom or large fill. The only accent shadow is `--ring-focus`, used for keyboard focus only.

### Motion
- Restrained and crisp. `--ease-out cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1)` (Linear-like), durations `120 / 200 / 360ms`.
- Hover = subtle: surfaces lighten to `--surface-hover`, cards lift `translateY(-2px)` + border brightens, links shift muted→primary. Primary button lightens (`lime-400`).
- Press = `translateY(1px)` (buttons), thumb slide (switch). No bounces, no spring, no decorative looping. Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Imagery
- Type-led brand — **no stock photography**. Where imagery is needed, use **editorial placeholder panels**: dark tonal gradient + faint grid + the mark watermark + a mono caption. Client/case visuals are represented as toned panels (e.g. deep green for Lacoste). When real assets exist, drop them into these slots full-bleed.
- Vibe of any photography (when supplied): cool, premium, high-contrast, architectural; neon-green light accents acceptable. Never warm corporate stock.

### Focus & states
- Focus ring is the lime ring (`--ring-focus`), not a browser outline.
- Selection is lime on ink.
- Disabled = 40% opacity, no pointer.

## ICONOGRAPHY

- **Deckhaus ships no proprietary icon set.** The brand's only iconographic asset is the **mark** — a quarter-circle "deck" with a leaf-shaped knockout (`assets/logos/deckhaus-mark-*.svg`), used as logo, watermark and favicon-scale glyph. It recolors to white / lime / black.
- **For UI icons, use [Lucide](https://lucide.dev)** — thin, geometric, rounded stroke icons that match the precise/Swiss tone. Use **stroke width ~2–2.2px, round caps/joins**, sized 16–20px in UI, inheriting `currentColor`. In this system a few inline Lucide-style strokes (arrow, menu) are hand-embedded in the website kit; consumers should pull the full set from the Lucide CDN.
  > **Substitution flag:** Lucide is a recommended stand-in, not a brand-supplied set. If Deckhaus has (or wants) a bespoke icon language, supply it and this section should be updated.
- **No emoji. No unicode glyph icons.** The only non-Lucide marks are: the brand mark, the mono numeric indices (`01`), and a short lime "tick" rule before eyebrows (drawn in CSS, not an icon).
- Arrows in CTAs are simple line arrows (`→` style, Lucide `arrow-right`), reinforcing forward motion ("present, launch, grow").
